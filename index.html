The system

One platform at the center, two autonomous systems built on the capabilities it proves out. The center is the product; the others are what it can do.

DreamBoxPlatform · centerpiece
Multi-agent AI platform

A sovereign, self-hosted AI platform. It started from one rule — keep the work private, nothing leaks out — and grew into a system that hosts AI agents, routes work between them, and runs as a coordinated fleet across multiple machines. If it can be reasoned through and built, this is where it gets built.

  • Intent-based agent orchestration — routes work to specialized agents on commit events, with a shadow-to-live cutover and instant rollback.
  • Retrieval (RAG) over a vector store, exposed to agents and editors through a Model Context Protocol (MCP) bridge.
  • Least-privilege agent design: per-agent tool allowlists, human-in-the-loop review before anything is published.
  • Model-selection evaluation and cost-aware routing — local generation gated by a frontier-model reviewer.

About

I spent twenty years keeping real environments running — healthcare, K–12 districts, medical-device manufacturing. Identity and access for thousands of users, fleet imaging and migrations, ERP go-lives where downtime had a price tag. I learned how systems actually behave when people depend on them.

Then I built my own. DreamBox is a working answer to a simple thesis: with the right tools and the room to use them, I can build the thing. It began as a private place to do AI work without anything leaking, and became a multi-agent platform that does real work on its own hardware. TradeBot and Dereth are what that platform makes possible — autonomous systems built to prove out decision-making, machine learning, and perception.

This is not a side project. It's a deliberate body of engineering work, built to a production standard, aimed at the problems companies are paying to solve right now.
